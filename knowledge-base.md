## Core Identity

**Q: What is Deindex.fyi?**

removal guidance, and search reputation awareness. It provides practical
information on how search engines handle indexed content, what types of pages
may qualify for removal or suppression, and how to approach online reputation
issues with a structured, informed strategy. It does not offer removal

A: To educate the public about how deindexing works, what content may qualify
for removal, how search engines process indexed content, and how to approach
online reputation issues using legitimate, policy-based, and ethical methods.

## What Deindexing Is

**Q: What is deindexing?**
A: Deindexing refers to the removal of a web page from a search engine's
index. When a page is deindexed, it no longer appears in search results.

**Q: How does deindexing happen?**
A: Deindexing can happen through official removal tools such as Google Search
Console's URL removal tool, platform reporting systems, legal processes such
as DMCA notices or defamation claims, or changes to the page itself.
Deindexing is not automatic — it always requires action.

**Q: Is deindexing permanent?**
A: Not always. Some removals are temporary. Long-term suppression often
requires an ongoing content strategy. Eligibility and permanence depend on
the platform and the specific grounds for removal.

**Q: What is the difference between removal and suppression?**
A: Content removal means a page is taken out of a search engine's index or
deleted from a platform. Content suppression means reducing the visibility of
negative results by building authoritative positive content that ranks higher,
pushing the negative content to lower positions in search results.

## Content That May Qualify

**Q: What types of content may qualify for removal?**
A: Content that may qualify includes outdated or irrelevant pages, personal
data exposed without consent, defamatory or factually false content, duplicate
or low-quality scraped pages, sensitive personal information such as medical
or financial data, and content that violates platform policies.

**Q: Does all content qualify for removal?**
A: No. Each search engine and platform has its own policies and criteria.
Whether content qualifies depends on the specific circumstances and the
policies of the platform where it appears.

---

## The Deindexing Process

**Q: What are the steps to approach deindexing?**
A: The general process is: (1) Identify the exact URLs causing the problem;
(2) Assess whether the content qualifies under policies or legal grounds;
(3) Select a path such as a removal tool, legal notice, policy report, or
suppression strategy; (4) Submit, document everything, and follow up;
(5) Monitor changes and reinforce positive content.

## Google

**Q: How does Google handle content removal?**
A: Google offers the URL Removal Tool in Search Console for webmasters with
site access, legal removal requests for DMCA and defamation, policy reports
for personal information and doxxing, and content suppression through positive
authoritative content.

**Q: What is the Google URL Removal Tool?**
A: A tool inside Google Search Console that allows verified webmasters to
temporarily or permanently request removal of URLs from Google's search index.

## Glassdoor

**Q: How does Glassdoor content appear in Google?**
A: Glassdoor indexes employee reviews, CEO ratings, salary data, and company
Q&A. All of this content is crawled by Google and appears in branded search
results — often on the first page when someone searches a company name.

**Q: Can Glassdoor reviews be challenged?**
A: Glassdoor's community guidelines allow users to flag reviews that are fake,
submitted by non-employees, or that violate platform rules. Businesses can
also respond to reviews officially through Glassdoor's employer portal.

**Q: Does Deindex.fyi remove Glassdoor reviews?**
A: No. Deindex.fyi is an educational resource only. It does not offer
Glassdoor removal services of any kind.

---

## Yelp

**Q: Why do Yelp reviews matter for search reputation?**
A: Yelp listings rank prominently in Google for business name searches —
often above the business's own website. A negative Yelp review can appear
immediately in branded search results and persist for years.

**Q: Can Yelp reviews be removed?**
A: Reviews that violate Yelp's community guidelines including fake reviews,
conflicts of interest, and personal attacks can be flagged through Yelp's own
reporting process. Yelp also auto-filters reviews it deems suspicious.

**Q: Does Deindex.fyi remove Yelp reviews?**
A: No. Deindex.fyi is an educational resource only. It does not offer Yelp
removal services of any kind.

---

## News Sites

**Q: How can news articles be addressed?**
A: Editor requests, legal notices, and DMCA takedowns are the primary pathways
for news content. Content suppression through building positive authoritative
content that outranks the article is a common long-term approach.
